Introduction to Prompt Caching

  • Prompt caching is a performance and cost optimization technique for Large Language Models (LLMs) by storing and reusing the processing results of unchanged prompt parts.
  • The main goal is to reduce latency and processing costs for long prompts by avoiding reprocessing unchanged parts between requests.
  • The basic mechanism is storing the model's internal state after processing the beginning of the prompt (input caching) or storing the entire prompt-response pair (output caching).
  • When an LLM processes a long prompt, most of the time and computational cost is spent on processing the beginning of the prompt, and prompt caching helps avoid reprocessing this part.
